# PM Brief — Free App Experience & Conversion

**What this is:** a polish-and-conversion pass over the free app now that the one-app foundation (Phases 0–3) is live on dev. We walk every free-user journey — the grandparent who never signs in, the parent who creates an account for score alerts, the coach who claims their team, the coach who upgrades, the organizer who sees "run your event here" — and make each step feel finished, with every paid-conversion moment legible and honest.

**Why now:** the connective tissue exists but was built phase-by-phase; nobody has walked the whole ladder end-to-end as a user. Doing this before the family phase means Phase 4 lands on a surface that already converts, and the privacy-review wait time gets used productively.

**Why it matters commercially:** this IS the funnel — fan → account (alerts) → coach claim → Premium ($29/mo team) and public event → organizer signup. Small seams here cost real conversions later; none of the pricing or gates change, only how clearly they're presented.

**How it runs (owner-ratified):** agents sweep first and fix mechanical issues silently; the owner only sees ranked judgment calls with labeled mockups. Then the owner walks short directed persona scripts on a real phone (the taste + push/install checks only a human can do), then free-roams last.

**What the owner will notice when it ships:** no dead ends or stale copy anywhere on the free surfaces; every "sign in / claim / upgrade / run a tournament" moment reads as a clear, honest offer at the moment of highest intent; and one stable answer to "how do I get back to Discover?" from anywhere.

**Out of scope:** tournament page internals (separate Tournament Mobile project), family features (Phase 4), any pricing/gating changes.

**Success looks like:** the owner completes all six persona scripts on a phone without hitting a single mechanical issue, and signs off on each conversion moment as "I'd tap that."
