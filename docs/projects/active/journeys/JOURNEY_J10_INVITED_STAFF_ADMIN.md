# J10 — Invited Org Staff Admin ("Alex") Journey Report

> Walked: 2026-06-13 | Method: code-walk (4-agent workflow, risk-targeted adversarial verify per owner decision) + live browser (26 screenshots, 1 design lens) | Tree state: feat/free-tier-coaches @ 5e4ad79 + uncommitted J4-001 fix (in scope); leverages the just-built Admin Role Parity | Status: draft | Findings routed: pending

**Context:** A checklist-style light journey. Alex is the owner's second-in-command, invited as `admin`. The day-one question is simple — *"what am I allowed to touch, and will the product tell me, or will I find out by hitting a wall?"* The locked Admin Role Parity policy (admin = owner **minus** billing & user-management; `manage_branding` now all-admin; member-management + org-settings owner-only) is the bar; findings that contradict the policy are policy/copy drifts, and the recurring theme is that the *boundaries are real but never communicated* — there is no orientation, and the walls are built from three inconsistent materials. The Admin Role Parity build closed most of the access-control gaps; what remains is the *experience* of being a scoped admin.

## The journey at a glance

From the inviter's chair the Members surface is genuinely good, and most of what the USER_MANAGEMENT UX plan flagged is already closed in this tree: the Role Guide matrix, the plain-language per-role descriptions, the seat banner that spells out the plan's seat math, the "Invite your first team member" empty state, the resend icon on pending rows. The invite modal is honest about what Admin/Staff/Scorekeeper can do, and both emails are clean and on-brand. But the moment the owner tries to staff a *real* club, two things break the promise. First, the owner wants to add the volunteer who already helps a neighbouring club — a normal Canadian-sports reality, and the exact human the platform's own coach-portal funnel creates (a coach with their own free workspace). The invite API hard-rejects any existing user who belongs to *any* other org with a blame-shaped 409 ("must be removed from their current organization first") — directly contradicting the multi-workspace `/home` switcher the platform is built around (J10-001). Second, on a Tournament-tier org the owner-only "Audit Log" button sits right there in the header but points at a route that has no page — it 404s, and even the org-path fallback would be bounced by `proxy.ts` (J10-003, the lens captured the bare developer 404).

The quieter inviter problem is trust: the member roster API (`GET /api/admin/members`) has **no capability check at all**. Any non-suspended member — a day-of scorekeeper, a staff operator, even a still-`invited` member who has only clicked their link — can fetch every member's email, full capabilities JSON, and last-sign-in time. The page hides the table behind a client-side gate so it *looks* locked down, but the data is one fetch away, while the sibling audit-log API is correctly owner-gated (J10-002). The polish is also undercut by copy that disagrees with the parity policy it ships alongside: the Role Guide matrix still tells the owner that admins cannot manage "Org settings & branding," even though branding is now an admin-default capability — the guide *understates* what the owner is about to grant (J10-004).

As the invitee, Alex's happy path is competent but the edges around stuck or partial invitees fail. Acceptance is *advisory*: `getAuthContext` excludes only `status='suspended'`, so an `invited` member with a session from the email link clears the admin layout gate and can hit admin APIs before ever completing the accept form (J10-006). The recovery routing is right in principle but rests on a failure window — `updateUser({password})` runs *before* the POST that flips the row active, so a failed POST leaves a member who has a password but an `invited` row, and the recovery route returns them to an accept form that demands they create a password that already exists (J10-007). The expired-link card offers only "Sign in" to a brand-new invitee who is passwordless by construction — a dead end with no in-product resend (J10-008). And the banned "Tournament Management Platform" descriptor is the first thing a brand-new user reads, twice, on a page wrapped in the full public-marketing nav/footer instead of a clean auth shell (J10-009, J10-027).

Day one as an admin, the product tells Alex almost nothing and the walls are a grab-bag. There is no role-aware welcome, no "you joined as an admin" banner, no role badge — `AdminChrome` has no first-session surface at all, so a correctly-scoped sparse tile set is indistinguishable from a broken feature (J10-015). On a Tournament-tier org the landing is reasonable (full ops + the real `manage_branding` parity), and the one owner-only thing — billing — is presented as a clean **locked card** ("Plan & subscription — Owner only," lock icon, click prevented). That locked-card pattern is the single best wall in the journey and the template the rest should adopt (J10-017) — but the product doesn't. Org settings and the audit log are *hard walls* (hidden + Access Denied panel); billing is *read-only* (visible + disabled + explained); and a league-role or treasurer teammate sails past the Members page gate, sees the full enabled "Invite Member" button, clicks it, and gets a bare one-word "Forbidden" modal because the write is gated on a capability the affordance never checked (J10-012, J10-013). Three surfaces, three contradictory philosophies. And there's a trap for the unlucky admin in a brand-new tournament-tier org with zero tournaments: the hub redirects them to onboarding, onboarding bounces any non-owner back to the hub, and the two ping-pong forever with no message — Alex's very first session can be an infinite redirect loop (J10-014).

Finally, the lifecycle exceptions are entirely invisible to the person they most affect. The owner's capability override editor is expressive (a three-way Role-default/Grant/Revoke per capability) but offers `billing` and `org_settings` as grantable when policy reserves them owner-only — false delegation confidence (J10-022). Suspend fires on one unconfirmed click (J10-018), and the suspended member's experience is a blind loop: they authenticate successfully, get bounced to login, re-enter a valid password, succeed, and loop forever with nothing distinguishing a suspended account from a network error (J10-019). Capability revocation and reinstatement send no notification and surface no in-app signal — the member discovers a vanished nav item mid-task or learns their access is back only by trying again (J10-020). The arc from grant → revoke → suspend → reinstate is something Alex lives through entirely in the dark.

## Top 5 moves

1. **Give the scoped admin one sentence of orientation and adopt the locked-card everywhere** — J10-015 (no role orientation), J10-016 (the settings-hub locked card is the one graceful boundary), J10-013 (three inconsistent wall styles). The single theme of the whole journey: the boundaries are correct, but nothing tells the admin what they are, so "correctly hidden" reads as "broken." A first-session role card + the visible-locked-labelled pattern on every owner-only surface fixes the checklist experience wholesale.
2. **Close the two access-control leaks the parity build left open** — J10-002 (ungated member roster API leaks every email + capabilities map to any member), J10-022 (owner-only `billing`/`org_settings` appear grantable, giving false delegation). Both undercut the trust model the Admin Role Parity work exists to establish.
3. **Stop trapping invitees at the edges** — J10-014 (fresh tournament-tier admin redirect loop, a true infinite loop on first session), J10-006 (acceptance is advisory — invited members reach /admin un-accepted), J10-007/008 (the failed-POST password re-create trap + the passwordless expired-link dead end). The happy path is competent; every *edge* around a stuck or partial invitee fails.
4. **Make member lifecycle visible to the member** — J10-018 (suspend fires unconfirmed), J10-019 (suspended → silent infinite login loop with no "you're suspended" message), J10-020 (revocation/reinstatement send no notification). A member should never live through grant→revoke→suspend→reinstate entirely in the dark.
5. **Fix the two day-one staffing breaks + the audit 404** — J10-001 (the multi-club 409 blocks the exact second-in-command the product's funnel creates), J10-003 (the Tournament-tier Audit Log button 404s). Both are the owner's *first* real actions when standing up a club, and both fail.

## Wow opportunities

- **A day-one "here's your access" card** (J10-015): reuse the role descriptions the owner already reads, shown once to the invitee — turns the checklist anxiety into a welcome.
- **Per-row override badges + an access map** (J10-024): a shield chip on members with custom capabilities, and a one-screen "who can do what" view — the owner-side auditability the override system implies but never surfaces.
- **A real pending/suspended status page** (J10-019, J10-026): distinct surfaces for "invited, not yet accepted" and "access paused" — closing the two states that currently render as crashes or loops.
