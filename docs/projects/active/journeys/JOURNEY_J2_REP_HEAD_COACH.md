# J2 — Rep Head Coach ("Dana") Journey Report

> Walked: 2026-06-13 | Method: code-walk (6-walker workflow, risk-targeted adversarial verify per owner decision) + live browser (28 screenshots, 2 design lenses: Basic+marketing and Premium) | Tree state: feat/free-tier-coaches @ 5e4ad79 + uncommitted J4-001 fix (in scope — J2's premium accounting suite is live again, walked as live surfaces) | Status: draft | Findings routed: pending

**Context:** The deferred full coach journey from Phase 2, walked now that the Premium rep-portal surfaces are stable and the J4-001 sync-params fix (uncommitted in this tree) has revived the premium accounting suite. Dana runs an independent rep softball team off her phone. The arc: marketing discovery → `/start/team` Basic standalone → team home (roster/schedule/fees/announcements) → ceiling/upgrade moments → Premium org-attached portal (lineups, budgets, dues). Two questions defined the walk — *can a coach even find the free product?* and *does the headline "lineup builder" exist where a coach can reach it?* Both answers surprised. Findings on Free-tier surfaces route into the in-flight `FREE_TIER_STRATEGY` / `COACHES_EXPERIENCE_EVAL` / `FREE_TIER_COACHES_*` plans rather than this backlog; STRIPE_INTEGRATION is archived, so billing items stay in-report (J4 precedent).

## The journey at a glance

The homepage actually speaks to Dana — a persona card asks "Coaching a single team?" and the Head Coach card lists exactly her pain: roster, "lineup builder exportable to PDF," budget, "works standalone — no organization account required." She taps "I'm a coach" and `/for-coaches` reads her mind again ("The roster lives in a group text. The lineup is in a notes app") — and then **closes every door**. The hero CTA, the $29/mo plan card, and the bottom CTA all open the same express-interest modal, which demands an Organization name she doesn't have and offers plan checkboxes for League and Club — not the Coaches Portal she clicked. The navbar's "Get Started" sends her to "Create Your Organization," an org on-ramp with no "just coaching a team?" exit (J2-002). The footer's "Coaches" link is the closest she gets to the truth — `/coaches/start` names "your free Coaches Portal" but never links it. Meanwhile, one URL she's never shown — `/start` — has a card reading "Coach a team · Free," wired to a real, built, working product (the fixture coach created Riverside Royals U13 through exactly this flow). The strategy plan *deliberately* stages this "marketing flip" for after Basic ships and instrumentation lands (Phase 8), so the hidden path is sequencing, not an accident — but Basic IS live and browser-verified, so today the platform's best coach-acquisition asset is invisible, the primary CTA mints wrong-shaped org accounts, and the only conversion action errors out by default (J2-001). The design lens drove the point home: `/for-coaches` "reads like a launch waitlist, not a live free product" and the coach-filtered pricing view shows **$39 before it shows Free** (J2-006, J2-008).

Once she finds `/start/team`, the on-ramp itself is excellent — name the team, create the account, land on the team home. But seams leak: coach signup skips email verification while org signup requires it, so a mistyped email is a confirmed-wrong, unrecoverable account (J2-010); a duplicate-email collision drops her typed team name on the login round-trip with no explanation (J2-011); and the portal shell hard-codes a "Tournaments" tab that dead-ends a coach who's never entered one (J2-013).

The Basic team home is honestly impressive for a free floor — a clean stat strip over Roster/Schedule/Fees/Announcements, consistent dark-and-lime, optimistic CRUD with revert-on-error, consent-gated PII. For a two-player demo it feels polished. Then Dana does the real job — load 14 players before the season — and the weekly loop strains. The roster is strictly one-at-a-time (open the 7-field form, save, re-click "Add player," ×14, with the whole list locked each time) — no paste, no CSV (J2-016). Fees inherit the same single-add rhythm, so charging one $75 tournament fee to 14 kids is 14 separate forms (J2-022). The schedule is a flat *ascending* list, so by week three her completed games pile up at the top and the next event is buried below weeks of history — only the stat strip's "Next" tile rescues her (J2-017). When she adds a game, the Opponent field — the one attribute that makes a row read "vs Mavericks" — is hidden two interactions deep behind an "optional details" toggle (J2-021). And comms is the riskiest moment: tapping "Send announcement" fires an irreversible email to every parent instantly, with no "send to 12 parents?" confirmation and no recipient preview; if two guardian emails have typos, the summary reports "Skipped: 2" with no way to learn *which* two (J2-018, J2-020). The design lens added that the whole team home "presents as a long linear list of database rows" with no hero, the warm Welcome announcement buried below the financial sections, and the Add actions rendered as text links rather than buttons (J2-019) — the free product that's meant to *win* the coach doesn't yet look like the polished tool she was promised.

The ceiling is the most honest upgrade surface in the product — lead-capture, "Basic stays free" everywhere, no fake paywalls — but it's wrapped in a promise it can't keep. Three surfaces and four marketing pages tell Dana her workspace "carries over automatically if your organization joins FieldLogicHQ," and the brand treats this as the platform's exclusive differentiator. **It is not true for the headline scenario:** the only code that carries a standalone basic team forward keys off a *tournament-registration* match, never an org-join — when her club joins Club and an admin makes her head coach of a rep team, her roster/schedule/fees do *not* carry over (J2-024). The promise the entire "organic path to Club" thesis rests on is undelivered. And when Dana *does* raise her hand — via the ceiling checkboxes or `/coaches/start` — she gets an inline "saved" and then silence: no confirmation email, no platform notification (J2-027).

The premium portal is the payoff, and it's the highlight of her journey — but the gateway to it is silent. Assigning a coach to a rep team (the door to the overview, schedule, lineup builder, and full accounting suite) sends **no email and no notification** — the inverse of the free floor, where creating a free team home *does* send a welcome (J2-025). Once inside, the marquee feature is missing in plain sight: the **lineup builder has zero navigation presence** — it's buried inside a game event's slide-over *below* the attendance form, with no nav item, an overview checklist that falsely marks "Prepare game lineups" complete on game-count (never reading a saved lineup), and a 680px grid that forces sideways scroll in a 720px mobile sheet (J2-031). The design lens confirmed it live: opening a game leads with attendance, and "Lineup — the marquee premium feature — is fully below the fold on both viewports." The accounting suite, dead until the J4-001 fix, comes alive as a genuinely capable bookkeeping cockpit (the strongest single oversight pattern in the product is its Upcoming Payables lanes) — but the franchise *voice* fights the franchise *mechanics*: the hub tells the coach "your org admin will set up dues schedules… before this view shows data," even though *she* owns dues and the same hub links her to set them (J2-033); the dues-reminder windows disagree (the hub promises 30/7-day emails, the only button she can press silently covers 3 days, J2-034); and marking a dues installment paid is one unconfirmed click that writes a permanent, non-reversible ledger entry (J2-035).

Net: the rails are right and several surfaces are genuinely strong (the on-ramp, the payables panel, the honest ceiling), but the journey leaks at the two moments that decide whether the free floor becomes the "organic path to Club" it's designed to be — the coach can't *find* the free product from marketing, and the free workspace doesn't actually *carry over* when she graduates — and the headline feature she was sold (lineups) is undiscoverable in the tier she'd pay for.

## Top 5 moves

1. **Let a coach find — and keep — the free product.** J2-001 (the live free product is unreachable from the entire marketing arc), J2-002 ("Get Started" mints the wrong account type), J2-024 ("carries over automatically" is undelivered for the org-join path). The free floor's entire "organic path to Club" thesis depends on a coach being able to *find* the free product and *keep* her workspace when she graduates — today she can do neither. The discovery half is sequenced behind the §13 instrumentation gate; the carry-over half is an unbuilt feature behind a shipped promise.
2. **Make the headline feature discoverable in the tier you'd pay for.** J2-031 (the lineup builder has zero nav, a false completion checklist, and a mobile-hostile grid), J2-025 (the assignment that grants the whole premium portal sends no notification at all). The marquee feature marketing sells is buried below an attendance form with no way to navigate to it, in a portal a coach is never told she has.
3. **Fix the Basic weekly loop for a real roster, not a demo.** J2-016 (single-add roster — 14 open/type/save cycles), J2-017 (the schedule buries the next event under weeks of history), J2-018 (the announcement blasts every parent on one unconfirmed tap), J2-022 (single-add fees). The free home is polished for two players and strains for fourteen — exactly the coach it's built to win.
4. **Make the free product look as credible as it is.** J2-019 (the team home reads as a list view, not a tool), J2-006 (`/for-coaches` reads like a waitlist), J2-008 ($39 before Free on the coach pricing view), J2-037 (the BvA empty state looks broken). The product is more capable than it looks; the visual layer is undercutting the conversion the strategy depends on.
5. **Make the premium money suite speak the franchise truth.** J2-033 (the hub tells the coach to wait for an admin to configure dues *she* owns), J2-034 (the dues-reminder windows contradict each other), J2-035 (Mark Paid is one unconfirmed, irreversible ledger write). The now-live accounting suite is the clearest justification for the paid tier — it just needs the franchise voice to match the franchise mechanics.

## Wow opportunities

- **First-run team home with identity** (J2-015 + J2-019 + J2-023): a team-colour hero + a "next event" callout + a first-run checklist turns the free home from a list view into *her team's* HQ.
- **Org-join import** (J2-024): when a club assigns a coach who owns a matching free team, offer to import her roster/schedule/fees into the rep team — delivering the carry-over promise the whole funnel rests on, and the single biggest retention moment in the journey.
- **A Lineups home** (J2-031): give the marquee feature a nav item and a per-game "Build lineup" affordance — the difference between a feature that's *sold* and one that's *used*.
- **Bulk roster paste** (J2-016): one paste-a-list box that turns the slowest moment in the product (onboarding 14 players) into one action — the cheapest possible win for the persona at the moment she decides whether to stay.
- **Reply-able, attributed parent comms** (J2-026): a reply-to + "From Coach Dana" header so the announcement reads as a note from their kid's coach, not faceless system mail — the core value of a phone-first comms tool.
