# J1 — Tournament Organizer ("Dana") Journey Report

> Walked: 2026-06-10 | Method: code-walk (17-agent workflow, adversarially verified) + live browser (40 screenshots, 5 design reviewers) | Tree state: feat/free-tier-coaches @ 412e403 + uncommitted in-flight work | Status: draft — awaiting owner checkpoint | Findings routed: pending

## The journey at a glance

Dana — a volunteer who runs one annual weekend softball tournament from her phone, Google Sheets, and a Facebook group — can complete the entire journey on FieldLogicHQ today, and the middle of it is genuinely good. The first-run wizard gets her from signup to a created event in about ten minutes; the Draft Launch Checklist is best-in-class guided sequencing; the public registration form, the scorekeeper PWA, and the schedule timeline with OVERDUE/NOW/NEXT chips are surfaces a volunteer org would be proud of. **Where the journey stalls is at its two emotional bookends — the first impression and the trophy moment — and in a cluster of trust-critical strings that are simply wrong.** The single biggest functional fix is making Sunday's bracket math trustworthy: a tied playoff score silently advances the away team (J1-083) and recording a coin toss doesn't re-seed an already-filled bracket (J1-084) — both can put the wrong team in the final of the one weekend the product exists for.

Discovery undersells a strong product. All three marketing surfaces are text cards on black — Dana never sees a schedule, bracket, or live scoreboard before being asked to trust her whole event to the platform (J1-004), and on mobile the landing page renders two giant blank voids because animated sections stay at opacity 0 (J1-002). The footer still says "FIELDLOGIC" with the retired "compete seriously" tagline (J1-003) and links to /docs, /status, and /contact — all 404s that the pricing FAQ explicitly promises (J1-001). Three route-map surprises shape everything downstream: **marketing never links the /start persona picker** (every CTA goes straight to /auth/signup — a documented Phase 2 state, J1-024); **tournament activation is reachable from three different surfaces** with two different definitions of "ready" (J1-032); and **the Founding Season comp auto-grants Plus at signup**, which silently changes which gates Dana ever sees — she gets schedule-publish emails, exports, and the generator without knowing why, and the free-tier walls (no exports, 3 seats, archive dead-end) are deferred to January 2027 rather than absent. If her verification email lands in spam, there is no resend path at all (J1-017).

The build loop is fast but hands her one wrong mental model that poisons the most schedule-critical input: the wizard's venue step never mentions fields or diamonds, so "Lions Park" with 3 diamonds becomes a single scheduling lane until she discovers the facility concept on a different page (J1-028). The checklist then deep-links her into Event Settings — six collapsed cards with zero information scent, which the design lens independently rated the worst scanability failure of the 40 screenshots (J1-029). Fees, required by the checklist, were never asked by the wizard (J1-030).

The public face is the product's best argument and its sloppiest details. The tournament site is shareable — but the exact URL shown in the activate modal at the go-live moment has a phantom `/tournaments/` segment and 404s for everyone who clicks Dana's Facebook post (J1-043). On game day the public home never shows the live game or a score (the hero leads with "Registration closed", J1-047), and /register still renders a fully open form during a closed event (J1-048). The acceptance email — the highest-stakes email of the journey — reads "accepted into the Division division for Tournament" and routes questions to FieldLogicHQ's own address (J1-065). The contact-privacy toggle is silently bypassed on home/news/rules (J1-045). Each of these is a small fix; together they are the trust story.

Game day splits in two. The volunteer-facing surfaces (scorekeeper, gate check-in, Results) are the strongest screens in the product — but the cockpit isn't live: dashboard gauges freeze while the activity feed beside them streams in real time, the live dashboard never shows which game is actually live (J1-085, J1-086), and there's no forfeit or bulk rain-delay tooling for the chaos that real weekends produce (J1-089, J1-091).

The close is where the journey quietly dies. The completed banner physically collapses on mobile (J1-105), champion chips are near-illegible light-on-lime (J1-106), the shared "Final Results & Standings" page never crowns the champion (J1-112), and the only standing action on the completed dashboard is "Archive" — whose confirm copy is wrong twice and omits that archiving 404s the public site (J1-103). For a free-tier organizer, archiving last year to create this year makes her history unviewable everywhere (J1-104) — at the exact moment the product should be proving year-over-year value and offering "Run it back" (J1-107).

## Top 5 moves

1. **Make Sunday's bracket math trustworthy** (J1-083, J1-084, J1-091, J1-076). A tied playoff score advancing the away team and a coin toss that doesn't re-seed can both crown the wrong champion on the one day the product exists for — and the publish control disappearing on the Playoffs stage compounds it. These are service-level fixes with clear repro paths; nothing else in the report matters if the bracket is wrong.

2. **Fix the five falsest strings in the journey** (J1-043, J1-065, J1-045, J1-103, J1-087). The go-live URL that 404s on Facebook, the acceptance email saying "the Division division for Tournament" with FieldLogicHQ's reply address, the bypassed contact-privacy toggle, the archive modal that's wrong twice, and the Results empty state promising realtime that doesn't exist. All are small, cheap, and pure trust — a volunteer who catches one of these stops believing everything else.

3. **Hand Dana the right mental model on day one** (J1-028, J1-029, J1-030, J1-032). One wizard question ("how many fields/diamonds?") makes her first generated schedule correct instead of one-third density; value summaries + anchor targeting fix the Event Settings wall the checklist keeps deep-linking into; a fee question closes the wizard→checklist gap and one definition of "ready" closes the three-surface activation gap.

4. **Make game day actually live** (J1-085, J1-086, J1-047, J1-097). The dashboard should show the live game with a score (the API has it, the desktop has an empty slot for it); gauges/Results/check-in should reuse the scorekeeper's existing realtime pattern; and the public home should lead with a live scorebug instead of "Registration closed". This is the "wow on game day" the platform already half-built — the public schedule's LIVE card proves the design language exists.

5. **Turn the close-out into the renewal** (J1-104, J1-105, J1-106, J1-107, J1-114, J1-111). Fix the collapsed mobile banner and illegible champion chips, make completion celebratory instead of amber-alert, give free orgs a way to see last year's results after archiving, and put "Run it back" one tap from the trophy moment. This leg decides whether Dana comes back next spring — most of it routes onto the already-open DASHBOARD_SUMMARY_IA plan.
