# J6 — Tournament Parent/Fan ("Jess") Journey Report

> Walked: 2026-06-11 | Method: code-walk (12-agent workflow: 6 leg-group walkers + risk-targeted adversarial verify per owner decision) + live browser (26 screenshots, 2 design reviewers + auditor harness notes) | Tree state: feat/free-tier-coaches @ uncommitted working tree | Status: draft | Findings routed: 2026-06-11 (PUBLIC_VISUAL_REDESIGN is archived/closed - the 32 routed findings stay in this report as the candidate successor fan-experience project for Phase 5 triage; backlog + fix-now items carried here)

**Context:** Jess is the persona the public tournament surface was built for — a parent who taps the coach's texted link in sun glare at the diamond, will never create an account, and asks exactly three questions: *is the game live and what's the score, when and where do we play next, did we make playoffs?* She is also the journey's only fully-anonymous persona, which is why this walk's worst finding matters so much. Fixtures: `/dev-test-org/live-demo` (in-progress, LIVE 1-1 game) and `/dev-test-org/completed-demo` (champions decided).

## The journey at a glance

Jess's journey runs on the most deliberately designed surface in the product — and one finding under it outranks everything else this audit has produced so far: **every public tournament page and the anonymous `/api/public/tournament-data` endpoint ship every team's coach name, coach email, payment status, and admin notes to every visitor's browser** (J6-001). The home page fetches teams with `{ admin: true }` and passes the raw rows into a client component's RSC payload; the verifier confirmed it end-to-end with curl (9 emails, `paymentStatus`, `adminNotes` returned anonymously) and found it *worse* than filed — `ScoreTicker` and `MyTeamDock` re-fetch the unsanitized payload client-side on every game-day pageview. If an organizer ever writes "hasn't paid, chasing cheque" in an admin note, it is publicly harvestable. Jess never sees it in the UI; one view-source by a parent is trust-destroying and a PIPEDA exposure. The verifier endorsed Blocker; under the audit taxonomy it files as High/trust-brand because the journey itself proceeds — but it is tagged **FIX-NOW** and the fix is a one-place public team sanitizer. Nothing else in this report should ship before it.

Above that fault line, the verdict is: **the delight layer is real and the truth layer is broken.** Both design lenses independently called the My Team dock the journey's hero — star, team name, red LIVE chip, rolling 1-1 score in one thumb-reach unit — and praised the schedule's LIVE row treatment, the standings' PENDING-chip + disclosure footnote, the amber CHAMPION panel, and the desktop rail-plus-ticker shell as architecturally right. But the product cannot agree with itself about what "live" means. LIVE is *score-submission* state, not game state: a 12:30 game at 12:40 is a plain scheduled row, then pulses LIVE from first score entry until midnight or admin finalization (J6-013). The team profile uses the *opposite* heuristic, so the page a coach-texted link most naturally lands on renders her kid's in-progress game as a finished tie — "T | 1–1", a T form pip, runs already banked — and never refreshes (J6-022). The game-detail page shows a pulsing LIVE badge over a facts panel reading "Pending score review", and threads that same workflow jargon into the share title and OG card (J6-019, J6-015). One game is simultaneously LIVE, pending, awaiting finals, and a counted tie depending on the surface. One shared `isGameLive` helper plus a single status-precedence model is the journey's biggest experience fix.

The follow loop — the product's no-account answer to "make this *my* tournament" — is exactly the right model with no front door and a broken side door. Nothing on home or game detail (her two likeliest landings) ever mentions following; the schedule's "Follow a team to pin its score" prompt is inert text (J6-011); and on the team profile, the most likely shared-link destination, the Follow button uses drifted local copies of `lib/follow.ts` that never dispatch `fl-follow-change` — so the flagship game-day dock silently never appears for her session, and unfollowing leaves a stale wrong-team dock (J6-010, verified independently by three walker groups; a one-file fix-now candidate). Even when she does follow, the payoff is withheld in places: standings compute a full your-team summary (rank, next game, latest score, division jump) and render none of it — dead code from a removed follow bar (J6-028) — and the bracket supports followed-team highlighting via a prop every public call site hardwires to `undefined` (J6-029).

The journey's edges leak trust in quieter ways. The register page and `/api/register` ignore the lifecycle gate entirely — mid-tournament, while the home hero says "Registration closed", the full wizard accepts a submission and mints a coach account (J6-035, fix-now candidate, confirmed by both design lenses). Score alerts — the marquee Plus fan feature — are literally undiscoverable on iPhone Safari: the toggle returns `null` where iOS needs an "install first" explainer, and the word "alerts" never appears anywhere in the iOS install flow (J6-048). In orgs requiring score finalization the "Final:" push never fires — fans only ever get an ambiguous "Score update" (J6-049). The installed "app" is a bookmark: `sw.js` has zero offline capability, which is precisely the redesign plan's own unshipped Phase-D item (J6-044). And the UTC-"today" family already filed as J5-025/J5-039 extends across eleven fan call sites — after ~8 PM Eastern the ticker vanishes mid-game and the dock dies on championship evening (J6-056, kept as a cross-ref with the full surface list for the shared local-date fix).

The finale fizzles where it should peak. Sunday night, the completed home offers "Final Public Record", an Event Summary of bookkeeping counts ("0 pending review", "0 unscored games"), a "Top Standings" card of *pool-play leaders* — which can headline a team that lost the final — and a leftover "Tournament Day — What To Check Now" panel insisting "No games are scheduled for today" (J6-052, J6-053). The string "Champion" appears nowhere on the page; the spotlight exists only inside the bracket, two taps deep. The champion's own profile still reads "POOL RANK 1st — In playoff spot" with zero trophy treatment — on the page parents screenshot (J6-025). Completed surfaces even show finals recorded against a team named "TBD" (J6-055). The emotional payoff of three days at the diamond — *who won, show grandma* — is the cheapest wow on this list, because the champion derivation, share buttons, and OG infrastructure all already exist.
