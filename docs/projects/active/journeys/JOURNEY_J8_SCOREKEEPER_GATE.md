# J8 — Scorekeeper ("Sam") + Gate Volunteer ("Gail") Journey Report

> Walked: 2026-06-13 | Method: code-walk (3-agent workflow, risk-targeted adversarial verify per owner decision) + live browser (18 screenshots, 1 design lens) | Tree state: feat/free-tier-coaches @ 5e4ad79 + uncommitted J4-001 fix (in scope); live-demo reseeded day-1 = today (2026-06-12) | Status: draft | Findings routed: pending

**Context:** A combined light journey for the two least-trained personas on the most stressful day — a parent handed a phone at the scoring table (Sam, role `official`) and a parent handed a phone at the gate (Gail, role `official` or `staff`). They were invited yesterday, got one email, and have never seen the product. The throughline: the field surfaces themselves are well-built on the happy path, but every place the day deviates from "one trained volunteer, one job, one org, never logs out" the persona is stranded with no signpost, no exit, and copy written for an admin.

## The journey at a glance

Sam's onboarding is, genuinely, the best in the product. The invite email is role-aware ("you'll have access to the scorekeeper app… after setup, you'll land directly in Scorekeeper View"), the accept-invite screen wears scorekeeper-flavoured copy ("Set Up Scorekeeper Access" / "Create Password & Open Scorekeeper"), and the redirect drops him straight onto `/{org}/scorekeeper`. The field surface is built for a phone in the sun: big lime score numerals, 42-44px touch targets, a bottom-anchored score sheet that falls under the thumb, and — the strongest single moment in the whole app — a policy-aware score flow that states the consequence *before* saving ("requires admin review" vs "becomes final immediately") and adapts its button label (Submit for Review / Finalize Score / Save Correction). On the merits of that one moment, the team nailed the brief.

But the promise inverts the instant Sam looks at the list. The word "assigned" appears across five touchpoints — the invite email, the added-user email, the role tooltip, the invite role description, and the in-app empty state ("No assigned games today") — and it is true zero times: an official with no assignment rows is *unrestricted* and sees every active tournament's entire game list, and there is no field-level or game-level assignment anywhere in the product (J8-003). On a multi-diamond weekend Sam is handed the whole event with no "happening now" anchor — every "To Score" card looks identical, and finding "my game" means reading every tiny mono metadata line (J8-006). The score sheet that reads so well in code is, on the actual phone, two near-invisible low-contrast boxes with no steppers, and the load-bearing "requires admin review" note is grey body text wedged between the blank inputs and the lime button — skippable in exactly the panic moment it exists for (J8-007, J8-008).

Gail's gate board is the same story: strong on the happy path (three big gauges, division-grouped rows, optimistic check-in that survives flaky field wifi, an Undo on every arrival action), quietly destructive when the day isn't tidy. The biggest hazard is the roster Edit — a jersey-and-name-only editor whose Save **deletes the team's entire roster and re-inserts it as `source='gate'`**, silently rewriting any coach-verified roster, dropping player notes on every save, and stripping DOB/position from any re-typed or newly-added player, with no confirmation (J8-010). The affordance that triggers it, "Add roster at the gate," renders as a faint text link that looks like a section label, not the destructive gate action it is (J8-011). Mark-paid is one-way where every arrival action is reversible (J8-016), the tournament picker never shows status so a volunteer can silently land on a draft or a locked completed event (J8-015), and a completed board dims every button to 45% opacity with no banner — the admin twin of the exact same board renders a clear read-only lock banner; the person least able to diagnose it gets the version without the explanation (J8-014).

Two failures turn ordinary mistakes into traps with no escape, and they bite the least-trained user hardest. First, **Sign Out is a dead 404 on both volunteer shells** — the link points at `/auth/logout`, a route that does not exist anywhere in `app/auth/`, while every other shell in the product signs out via the `signOut()` client call (J8-001). On a borrowed or shared gate-table phone, the one control a volunteer understands gets a 404 in developer-diagnostic language ("[DIAGNOSTIC]: ROUTE_NOT_FOUND"), leaving their session live on someone else's device. Second, the cross-org login loop: a member of org A who opens org B's volunteer URL gets a null context, is bounced to login, re-enters a perfectly valid password, authenticates successfully, and is pushed *straight back* to the same URL — forever, because the login page has no already-authenticated forward (J8-018).

The capability model is actually generous — one `official` can both score *and* check teams in — but the wayfinding never honours it. The two shells never cross-link; nobody is ever told the `/check-in` URL exists; an official who guesses the obvious `/{org}/admin` lands on a blank zero-tile hub with no redirect (J8-019); and the role-to-surface mapping is backwards — the `official` gets a blank admin hub while a `staff` gate volunteer gets the *full* tournament dashboard with schedule-health conflict panels they have no use for (J8-021). The product was built for one volunteer doing one job and never logging out; the moment reality deviates, the two safety rails every other shell has (a working sign-out, an authenticated-login forward) are simply missing here.

## Top 5 moves

1. **Give the volunteer shells a working exit and re-entry** — J8-001 (Sign Out is a 404 on both shells, Blocker), J8-002 (session-expiry strands the volunteer with no sign-in control), J8-018 (cross-org URL = infinite login loop). These are the three ways a volunteer gets permanently stuck on a borrowed phone; all three are missing safety rails every other shell already has.
2. **Fix the score-entry moment for an untrained thumb** — J8-007 (invisible inputs, no steppers), J8-008 (the "requires admin review" note is skippable grey text), J8-006 (no "now" signal — can't tell which game is live). The save-*semantics* are the best in the app; the save-*surface* on a real phone in the sun undoes that.
3. **Make the gate roster non-destructive** — J8-010 (Edit deletes the whole roster and rewrites coach provenance / drops DOB/position/notes), J8-011 ("Add roster at the gate" is an invisible link that triggers it). A volunteer fixing one jersey number can silently wipe a coach's compliance data with no warning.
4. **Stop dropping volunteers at the wrong door** — J8-019 (official → blank admin hub), J8-021 (role mapping backwards: staff gets the full dashboard), J8-020 (/home has no gate path), J8-003 (the "assigned games" copy that sets a false mental model). The capability model lets one official do both jobs; the wayfinding never honours it.
5. **Make the gate board honest about event state** — J8-015 (picker can default to a draft/locked event with no status shown), J8-014 (completed board dims silently with admin-voiced 409 copy), J8-016 (mark-paid is the one irreversible action). The board is sharp where the day is tidy and quietly wrong where it isn't — which at a gate table is most of the time.

## Wow opportunities

- **A "happening now" field HUD** (extends J8-006/009): the scorekeeper list should open on the live/next game with a LIVE stripe and a countdown, not a flat chronological list of identical cards — the field analogue of the admin game-day board.
- **One-tap job switch** (extends J8-020): an official who can both score and check in should get a single toggle between the two shells, honouring the capability the model already grants.
- **Thumb-stepper score entry** (J8-007): port the admin B7 −/+ steppers to the field sheet — the single change that makes scoring genuinely one-thumb.
- **Day-of volunteer hand-off** (cross-ref J1-080's QR kit): a printable per-diamond QR that deep-links a volunteer straight to the right scorekeeper/gate surface, bypassing the wrong-door maze entirely.
