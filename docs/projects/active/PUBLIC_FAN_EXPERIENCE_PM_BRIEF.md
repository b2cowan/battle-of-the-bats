# PM Brief — Public Fan Experience

> **Created:** 2026-06-13 · **Companion plan:** [PUBLIC_FAN_EXPERIENCE_PLAN.md](PUBLIC_FAN_EXPERIENCE_PLAN.md) · **Source:** User Journey Audit Phase 5 (FP-2), successor to the archived Public Visual Redesign

**What it does:** Makes the public tournament pages — the ones a coach texts to parents — tell the truth and finish the story. A parent's three questions are *is the game live and what's the score, when and where do we play next, did we make playoffs* — plus the payoff at the end: *who won, show grandma*. The visual redesign already built the beautiful pieces; this project makes them honest and connects them.

**Why it matters:** The journey audit found a real delight layer sitting on a broken truth layer. The same game can read as live on one screen, "pending review" on another, and a finished tie on a third; the "follow your team" feature has no front door and a broken side door, so the flagship live-score dock often never appears; the page a shared link most naturally lands on shows an in-progress game as already over; and on Sunday night the completed page never says who won — the champion is buried two taps deep. Score alerts, the marquee paid feature, are invisible on iPhone. The Public Visual Redesign that built these surfaces is archived, so none of this has an owner.

**Who benefits:** Tournament parents and fans (the no-account audience the public surface exists for), and the organizers whose events look more credible and shareable — plus the Plus-tier upsell that score alerts represent.

**Expected impact:** One consistent "live" everywhere; a follow loop that's findable and keeps its promise; a game-day home that answers all three questions at a glance; a finale that crowns the champion and invites the share; and score alerts that actually work on the dominant parent platform.

**Priority:** High — public pages are the platform's most-shared surface and the cheapest, highest-emotion wow on the backlog (the champion moment reuses infrastructure that already exists). Ships after the FP-1 integrity work that removes the public PII leak.

**Success criteria:**
1. A single game shows one consistent state across every public surface.
2. Following a team from a shared link summons the live-score dock and highlights the team in the bracket.
3. The completed page crowns the champion and offers the share.
4. iPhone fans can find and turn on score alerts.
5. The 32 unshipped design items from the archived redesign are shipped or explicitly deferred.
