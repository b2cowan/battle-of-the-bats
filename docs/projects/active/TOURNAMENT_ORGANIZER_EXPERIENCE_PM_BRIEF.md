# PM Brief — Tournament Organizer Experience

> **Created:** 2026-06-13 · **Companion plan:** [TOURNAMENT_ORGANIZER_EXPERIENCE_PLAN.md](TOURNAMENT_ORGANIZER_EXPERIENCE_PLAN.md) · **Source:** User Journey Audit Phase 5 (FP-5), new project

**What it does:** Fixes the tournament organizer's end-to-end experience — the persona the product was originally built for. The audit found the middle of her journey genuinely good; it stalls at the two emotional bookends (the first impression and the trophy moment) and in a cluster of trust-critical strings that are simply wrong. This project makes Sunday's bracket math trustworthy, fixes the false strings, hands the organizer the right mental model on day one, and makes game day actually feel live.

**Why it matters:** The organizer journey produced the most findings of any persona (118). The most important: a tied playoff score silently advances the wrong team, and recording a coin toss doesn't re-seed the bracket — both can crown the wrong champion on the one day the product exists for. Around that, a cluster of small-but-trust-destroying details: the go-live URL the organizer copies to Facebook 404s; the acceptance email says "accepted into the Division division for Tournament" and routes questions to the platform's own inbox; the archive confirmation is wrong twice. And the bookends underdeliver — the wizard never explains the field/diamond model so the first schedule is one-third density, and the live dashboard never shows the live game.

**Who benefits:** Tournament organizers (volunteer and professional) across the Free/Tournament and Tournament Plus tiers — the platform's founding audience.

**Expected impact:** A bracket that can't crown the wrong team; trust-critical strings that are all true; a wizard that sets the organizer up correctly on day one; and a game day that shows the live game with a live score.

**Priority:** High — the bracket-math correctness is fix-now-worthy (it decides champions), and this is the largest single block of unowned findings.

**Success criteria:**
1. A tied or coin-toss-decided playoff can't crown the wrong team; publishing works in every stage.
2. The five worst strings in the journey are all true.
3. The wizard explains fields/diamonds and asks about fees before the checklist blocks on them.
4. The live dashboard shows the live game with its score.
