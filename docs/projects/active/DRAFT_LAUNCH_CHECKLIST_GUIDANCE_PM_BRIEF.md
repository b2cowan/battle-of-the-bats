# PM Brief — Draft Launch Checklist: Guidance & Discovery

**One line:** Turn the pre-launch setup checklist from a flat "optional" junk drawer into a guided tour that tells a first-time organizer what's worth a look before going live — and quietly shows off settings they didn't know they had.

## Why it matters
First-time tournament organizers are often volunteers. Today, once they've set dates and a division, everything else is dumped into one collapsed "Optional setup" drawer that nobody opens — so they never discover that game timing, tie-breakers, venues, and the public page even exist as things to set. They go live with empty pages or default rules they never consciously chose. We want the screen to *teach* and *reassure*, not just gate.

## What the organizer sees differently
- The "Optional setup" drawer is renamed to **name what's inside** — *"Schedule, venues, tie-breakers & more"* — so the recognition ("oh, we should set tie-breakers") happens before they even click.
- Open it and the items are now **grouped and labeled**: *Recommended before go-live* (schedule, venues, public page, rules), *Defaults you can fine-tune* (game timing, tie-breakers, contact), and *Registration & fees — your call*.
- The default-backed settings now **show their current value right on the row** — "Game timing: 90 min / 15 min buffer," "Tie-breakers: H2H → Run Diff → …" — so a user instantly sees what's in effect and can tweak it. A small "?" explains the concept in two sentences without leaving the page.
- The counter reads "**reviewed**," not "complete" — a default left as-is isn't unfinished homework.
- A schedule with real conflicts **floats to the top** of the checklist (only when there's an actual problem) instead of hiding in the drawer.
- When they click **Activate**, the confirmation now shows a quick "here's what you're going live with" summary — what's still unset and which defaults will apply — so there are no surprises, without adding a blocking step.

## What does NOT change
- The bar to go live is unchanged: dates + at least one division. Nothing new is *required*. Fees and online registration remain optional choices.

## Tradeoffs
- We deliberately avoided extra banners, a second confirmation step, and any red/warning styling on default items — those nag and erode trust. The guidance rides on grouping, plain labels, and visible defaults instead.

## Success criteria
- Organizers open the drawer more often (named label) and adjust defaults like game timing/tie-breakers before launch instead of discovering them mid-event.
- Fewer tournaments go live with no venues / empty public page unknowingly (caught by the go-live summary).
- No increase in "I'm stuck, what's required?" confusion — the required gate stays at two items.

## Priority
Medium — a polish/discovery win on the most-used organizer screen; low effort, high reuse of existing components. Builds on the FP-5 Tournament Organizer Experience work.
