# PM Brief — Coach Portal Growth (Free→Paid Education + Cross-Shell Continuity)

> Companion to [COACH_PORTAL_GROWTH_PLAN.md](COACH_PORTAL_GROWTH_PLAN.md). Created 2026-06-17.

## What this is
Two related improvements to how the **free** Coaches Portal connects to the **paid** one:
1. **Tell coaches what the paid version adds — on the page where it's relevant** — instead of leaving them to guess what they're missing.
2. **Make the free and paid experiences feel like the same product** — so upgrading doesn't feel like switching apps.

## Why it matters
The free Coaches Portal is an **acquisition wedge**: a coach who runs their team here is the warmest possible lead for the paid standalone Coaches Portal and for their org adopting Club. Today the free tool doesn't explain what's beyond it (on the working pages), and the free→paid jump looks like a different brand. Both leak conversion and trust.

## What the coach sees differently

**Feature education (Phase 1, shipping now):** at the bottom of each tool page (Roster, Schedule, Fees, Announcements) — *below* their actual work, never a popup — a quiet line names the 2–3 specific things the paid Coaches Portal adds there (e.g. on Fees: dues automation, email reminders, a season budget), reassures that the free tool stays free, and offers an "Express interest" link. It can be dismissed to a tiny "what this adds →" link so it never nags.

**Brand continuity (Phase 1, shipping now):** the admin screens now lead with the same "FL" mark as the coach portal, and a coach who also helps run an org always has a one-click "Coaches Portal" door back to their team space.

## What stays the same (deliberate)
The coach portal stays warm and simple; the tournament admin stays a dense control panel. Those are two correct *personalities* for two different jobs — we're aligning the *brand wrapper*, not flattening either one.

## Customer impact
- Coaches discover the paid value at the moment it's relevant, without feeling the free tool is crippled.
- The upgrade path captures interest today (self-serve checkout opens in a later phase).
- A coach who upgrades or also runs an org feels like one continuous product, not three.

## Priority & success criteria
- **Priority:** medium-high — directly improves the free→paid funnel that the free Coaches Portal exists to feed.
- **Success:** express-interest submissions from the per-page strips; no rise in free-portal abandonment (the strips must not nag); qualitative "feels like one app" across the upgrade.

## Tradeoffs
- We do **not** open self-serve coach checkout yet (gated) — CTAs capture interest. Honest, and avoids a dead-end purchase flow.
- We do **not** unify the coach/admin visual languages — only the brand chrome — to avoid a worse, blended middle.
