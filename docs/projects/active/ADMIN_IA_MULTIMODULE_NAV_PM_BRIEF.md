# PM Brief — Admin IA & Multi-Module Navigation

> **Created:** 2026-06-13 · **Companion plan:** [ADMIN_IA_MULTIMODULE_NAV_PLAN.md](ADMIN_IA_MULTIMODULE_NAV_PLAN.md) · **Source:** User Journey Audit Phase 5 (FP-7), new project

**What it does:** Makes the admin product stop acting like a tournament tool when it's running a league or a club. Today the dashboard, navigation, public homepage, and the `/home` switcher all lead with tournaments regardless of what the organization actually does. This project makes the org-level interface plan-aware — it orders itself by what the org runs.

**Why it matters:** The audit found the same skew from four different personas. A club president's dashboard is seven doors and a rocket whose only call-to-action is tournament setup; his public homepage leads with "No active tournaments right now — check back soon!" about fifty weeks a year, above his always-on league, while his rep program is publicly invisible. A house-league admin's mobile nav points at tournament surfaces. A league parent's first impression of the org homepage is an empty-tournament apology. The `/home` switcher greets a volunteer with "1 ACCESS AREA FOR OWNER@…". The platform doesn't yet know who its league and club customers are.

**Who benefits:** League and club admins and their public visitors — the higher-tier customers whose orgs are mostly *not* about tournaments.

**Expected impact:** Dashboards, navigation, and public homepages that lead with what the org actually runs; a `/home` switcher that speaks like a product; and buried money surfaces that are reachable from their own sidebars.

**Priority:** Medium-High — it's the connective tissue that makes the league and club tiers feel like products built for them, and it spans every higher-tier persona.

**Success criteria:**
1. A league/club org leads its dashboard, nav, and public homepage with what it actually runs.
2. The `/home` switcher speaks to a user, not an operator.
3. Buried module surfaces are reachable from their sidebars.
4. A tournament can't be slugged to shadow an org-level route.

**Scope note:** Day-one role orientation stays with the Admin Role Parity plan; this project owns the navigation half. It can be folded into the per-tier projects if the owner prefers fewer spin-outs.
