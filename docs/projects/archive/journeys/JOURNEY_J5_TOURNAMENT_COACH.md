## The journey at a glance

Marcus — a rep coach who registers his Bears U11 into three tournaments a season and lives on his phone — can complete this journey today, and the engineering underneath it is honest in a way that's rare: opponent names only show when the organizer has truly published them, the coach's fee math reuses the organizer's own resolver, and his Follow toggle shares the exact localStorage key the public fan dock uses. **The verdict: the rails are right and the moments are missing.** Whichever of the two entry points Marcus takes — self-registering on the public form (the best form in the product, with inline account creation) or being added by Dana and claiming via an email link — both converge on a portal that works but never *feels* like game day, fee day, or championship day. The phase-adaptive hero that 5h shipped adapts its *copy*, not the *experience*: prep, fee-owed, game day, and champions-crowned are pixel-identical templates distinguished by one small monospace line (J5-032), and the design lens scored the one day that matters — game day — as the flattest screen in the portal while the *public* team page two taps away out-game-days the coach's own HQ (J5-041, J5-044).

Arrival is the roughest leg. An admin-added coach who signs up lands on a claim wall — on the live fixture, ~16,000px of identical "Claim team" cards with the live tournament buried mid-list, and after claiming, "YOUR TEAMS" renders *below* the remaining wall on a page still headed "CLAIM YOUR TEAMS" (J5-013). Nothing anywhere in the portal says a tournament is happening *now*: no LIVE badge, the in-progress event sorts below a July one, and every card wears the same green ACCEPTED chip (J5-014). One of the three nav tabs — "Teams" — opens a Premium upsell page instead of his team (J5-015), and the auditor's live-DB check found the sharpest edge: orphaned basic teams whose registrations are permanently inaccessible, invisible to claim discovery, *and* unclaimable — a limbo reachable in prod via user deletion (J5-012).

Two distinct UTC bugs poison trust at the worst times. First, date *rendering*: every Team HQ phase and the records list display the tournament a day early — screenshots show "Jun 9 – Jun 11" for a Jun 10–12 event while the game rows on the same page are correct (J5-025). Second, date *logic*: "today" is computed in UTC, so at 8:00 PM Eastern an in-progress evening game stops looking live, and on the final evening the phase flips to "result" mid-final — killing the 30s poll and freezing the LIVE scorebug while Marcus's team is still batting (J5-039). The walk also surfaced a genuine money bug the design lens almost wrote off as a fixture problem: two of the three organizer "Mark Paid" paths write only `payment_status='paid'`, which the shared fee resolver deliberately ignores whenever a fee schedule exists — so the organizer's page says **paid** while Marcus's portal says **OWED · $500.00 due Jul 10** (J5-026).

The bookends are the emotional gaps. After winning the tournament 5–0, Marcus opens his HQ to "You're in!", "Event complete", and a NOT ARRIVED check-in chip styled as an unfinished task (J5-049, J5-052) — no trophy, no record, no standings link, no share. That's slice 5m's scoped job, and this walk confirms it with two hard additions: the afterglow must render independent of the *organizer's* plan (today the only post-event touch, the results email, is Plus-gated — free-org coaches get total silence, J5-050), and every "Express interest" ask currently dead-ends on a gated Stripe checkout that says "signup is not open yet" over a disabled button (J5-051).

The email layer carries the journey between visits and ships two verified Highs: organizers who accept/reject from the Teams surface send coaches "accepted into the **Division** division for **Tournament**" — literal placeholder names (J5-056) — and both bulk paths route "Questions? Contact the organizer" to **fieldlogichq@gmail.com**, the platform bootstrap inbox (J5-057). The journey's first email still tells a coach who *just* created an account to "Create a free FieldLogicHQ account" (J5-002), and no game-day reminder exists at all (J5-058, 5m's deliverable — content spec now written below). The single biggest fix in the journey: anchor the date/"today" handling to one America/Toronto helper across the coach surfaces (J5-025 + J5-039) — it repairs the live scorebug, the phase flips, the off-by-one ranges, and the Active/Past bucketing in one move, and everything 5j–5o builds on top of it.
