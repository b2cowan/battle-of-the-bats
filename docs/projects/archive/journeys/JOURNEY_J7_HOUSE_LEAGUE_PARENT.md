## The journey at a glance

Priya — a parent who arrives at 9:40 PM from a link in a community Facebook group, registers her 9-year-old, e-transfers $165 "if someone tells her how," and then lives on the schedule page every Tuesday for four months — meets a journey with two utterly different halves. **The front half is genuinely good**: the registration form is the best screen in the league module (single column, boxed sections, big targets, a readable two-sentence waiver, live "Open — 10 spots remaining" division labels), the capacity bars give honest urgency, the submit branching (approve/pending/waitlist) is correct with a closed-season POST properly 409ing, and the account-less status lookup — one email field, no login — is arguably the most parent-friendly pattern on the whole platform, with the right emotional headline per status ("Confirmed", "Waitlisted · #1", "Pending Review"). **The back half — in-season life, the team question, payment, season close — is missing or unreachable.** The product registers her daughter beautifully and then loses the family.

The headline is a Blocker: **the in-progress season vanishes from the league index and the org home the moment next season's registration opens** (J7-001, cross-ref J3-067). Both surfaces have exactly one "Current Season" slot and a Past list that admits only completed/archived seasons, so during the completely normal annual overlap — 2027 registration opens mid-2026-season — the season her daughter is actually playing in, with its 18 registrations, 6 teams, games, and standings, has **zero links anywhere in public navigation** (live curl confirmed). Her two goals on the same visit — "register for next year" and "when's the game this week" — collide, and one season erases the other. Until this lands, every other in-season fix in this report is invisible for the months a league is busiest.

Beneath the Blocker, the journey's three central questions are all structurally unanswerable — not by admin negligence, but because the product has no field, no send, or no column to answer them with. **"How do I pay the $165?"** — the only payment copy in the entire loop is "payable separately to {org}"; the pending email promises instructions at approval and the approval email says nothing; `league_seasons` has no payment-instructions field while tournaments do (J7-008). **"What team is she on, who's the coach?"** — team assignment is a silent column write: no email fires, the status page deliberately doesn't select `team_id`, and `coach_name` renders on zero public surfaces; the lens found a placed child's card showing "CONFIRMED · Division A · Ref: 069806EA" — the internal reference code gets pride of place while the most-asked question in youth sports is absent (J7-016). **"Who do I even ask?"** — the status page's contact-us mailto reads `organizations.contact_email`, a column that exists in *neither dev nor prod*, so the failure-path help is structurally dead code and she is always told to "contact your organization admin" with no address (J7-012).

The in-season weekly loop J3 already mapped from the admin lens, and the parent walk confirms the blast radius: the schedule never says *where* (location is stored on every game and dropped on the floor — J3-071), cancelled games silently vanish while postponed games show their stale original time (J3-070), practices don't exist publicly (J3-052), and nothing is ever sent automatically — no team reveal, no schedule-published, no cancellation notice (J3-062/064). The funnel's two ends also never meet: every page from last season — her bookmark, the old Facebook link — is a terminal dead end that never mentions the registration that's open *right now* (J7-022), and season close renders "This season has concluded." with a lime CTA that can land a parent on "No divisions have been set up for this season yet." (J7-023).

The single biggest fix is the Blocker (one render change across two files); the single biggest *trust* fix is giving the $165 a "how." And the highest-leverage wow is already half-built: the account-less status card, the journey's best container with its thinnest payload, wants to become a **team card** — team, coach, next game, venue — fed by a team-reveal email ("Nora is a Cardinal! Coach Pat, first game June 14") that would be the league's most-screenshotted message and its organic ad in the very Facebook group Priya came from.
