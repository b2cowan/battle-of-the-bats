# J9 — House-League Team Coach ("Pat") Journey Report

> Walked: 2026-06-13 | Method: code-walk (2-agent workflow, risk-targeted adversarial verify per owner decision) + live browser (18 screenshots, 1 design lens) | Tree state: feat/free-tier-coaches @ 5e4ad79 + uncommitted J4-001 fix (in scope) | Status: draft | Findings routed: pending

**Context & owner direction:** This light journey is the receiving end of J3's "communicate to coaches." The walk confirmed the premise the audit suspected: **the platform has no surface for a house-league coach** — he exists only as `league_teams.coach_name`, a bare optional TEXT column. Per the owner's standing direction, this report does **not** stop at documenting the gap. After establishing the gap (the coach-angle defects, J9-001…J9-009), it pivots to a constructive **UX-upgrade recommendation set** (J9-010…J9-014): for each real job Pat has, the recommendation names the *existing platform primitive* nearest to serving it, so each reads as "extend X," not "invent Y." The structural-root findings (coach has no identity, no comms channel, no portal) are **J3-061's** — already routed to `FREE_TIER_STRATEGY_PLAN.md §16` — and are cross-referenced here, not re-filed.

## The journey at a glance

Pat coaches the Cardinals on Tuesday nights. He's a volunteer parent, not an administrator, and what he needs is small and concrete: his roster with guardian phone numbers, the practice and game schedule, a way to tell families when Tuesday is rained out, maybe a place to report a score, and at season's end his team's record to celebrate. Walked as Pat, the most striking thing isn't a broken screen — it's the complete absence of any screen at all. Pat has no identity in this platform. He is a string: `league_teams` is `id, season_id, division_id, name, color, coach_name, sort_order, created_at` and nothing else — no `coach_email`, no `coach_phone`, no `user_id`, no FK. The only reachable humans in the entire house-league data model are the guardians on `league_registrations`, so the platform can reach Pat only if he happened to register his own kid, and even then only as a parent.

Both front doors a coach might use are dead ends. If his org adds him as a member, the natural role is `coach` — which grants exactly one capability, `module_rep_teams`, useless for house league. He lands on `/{org}/coaches` and the org-scoped portal resolves a coach's teams *exclusively* from `rep_team_coaches`; `league_teams` is invisible to it. So Pat sees a faded person icon, "No team assignments yet," and "You'll appear here once your org admin assigns you to a team" — a promise that can never be fulfilled, because there is no rep team to assign him to (J9-002). The design lens scored that empty state as a "blank black void that looks broken, not designed" (J9-006). The free org-less `/coaches` portal is no better — it's built entirely on `basic_coach_teams` and tournament teams, with zero references to any league table. House league is the only module on the platform whose franchise operator has no operator surface.

His one window is the anonymous public league site, and it's built for a parent checking on one child, not the operator of a team. The schedule prints who plays and when, and that is all: the design lens confirmed live that the public schedule shows **no game location** even though the admin sets it (the same Maple Grove Diamond 1/2 the admin schedule renders prominently), and a game the admin marked **postponed renders identically to a live one** — same dark card, no badge, no tint — so Pat would drive to a field for a game that isn't happening (both cross-ref J3-071/J3-070, screenshot-confirmed here). There is no team filter, so to find Cardinals games Pat reads every card across every week (J9-001). The standings page — his only season-end artifact — is the same anonymous division table every parent sees, with no coach name and, on mobile, the most meaningful column (DIFF) clipped off the right edge with no scroll cue (J9-005, J9-009). The status page is keyed on guardian email, so his own email returns nothing.

And what reaches Pat through league comms is nothing, by construction. The only house-league comms tool emails registration *guardians*; even its "team" scope resolves to that team's guardians, never the coach; there is no coach option in the audience selector (the lens confirmed the broadcast composer offers only All / Waitlist / Pending). The one email a coach might be forwarded — `leagueBroadcastHtml` — is a branded shell with **zero links** that silently drops its `contactEmail` param, so it leads nowhere and offers no reply path (J9-004). On rainout night, the product loses to the group chat it meant to replace.

**But the gap is shallow, and that is the point of this report.** Every job Pat has is one short wire from a primitive the platform already owns. His roster with guardian contacts is fully structured in `league_registrations` per team — the admin already exports it as XLSX/CSV with DOB, guardian name, email, and phone; only the delivery to the coach is missing (J9-011). His practice schedule lives in `league_practices`, a real, team-scoped, recurrence-aware table with a full admin CRUD UI — built, and unservable to the one person who runs the practice (J9-012). His rainout is a `league_games` status flip the admin can already make; the gap is purely notification fan-out, and `lib/notify.ts` is a complete dispatch with an explicit `userIds` parameter waiting to be called (J9-013). Score reporting has a proven analogue in the tournament scorekeeper/official submission pattern (J9-014). And the identity problem that gates all four has **three battle-tested solutions already in the tree** — the `coachPortalUrl` claim-by-email link, the `basic_coach_team_users` claim, and the `rep_team_coaches` user-linked assignment (J9-010). The infrastructure for a house-league coach portal is almost entirely written; it is simply pointed at rep teams and tournaments. The recommendation is therefore not "build a coach product" — it is "add `coach_email` to `league_teams`, reuse the lightest claim pattern, and aim the existing portal, practices read, notify wire, and score pattern at the league table." The League Starter free floor already grants `module_house_league` and even comments that practices and games are "owner-only internal scheduling" today — naming the exact seam where Pat's surface belongs.

## Top 5 moves

The owner direction makes the recommendation set the headline, so the Top 5 leads with what a house-league coach *should get*, each grounded in a primitive that already exists.

1. **Give the coach an identity (the keystone)** — J9-010: add `coach_email` to `league_teams` and reuse the proven claim-by-email pattern (`coachPortalUrl`). This one column plus any one of three battle-tested claim flows turns Pat from a TEXT string into a working login, and it is the prerequisite that unlocks every other move. (Structural root is J3-061 / FTS §16; this is the named implementation path.)
2. **Deliver the roster the admin already exports** — J9-011: render a read-only team roster with guardian contacts on the coach's portal, scoped to his `team_id`, reusing the exact columns the admin export already serializes. The single most-needed artifact, a query away.
3. **Surface the practice schedule he's expected to run** — J9-012: a coach schedule tab reading the already-built, team-scoped, recurrence-aware `league_practices` table. Built today, unservable to its only consumer.
4. **Wire rainout to the notify dispatch that already exists** — J9-013: one `notify()` call on a game status flip, reaching the coach (and optionally guardians) by `userIds`. On rainout night this is the difference between the product and the group chat.
5. **Fix the two coach-facing defects that exist *today*, before the portal lands** — J9-002 (the "No team assignments yet" dead end that misleads any coach onboarded the obvious way) and J9-003 (the module-cap over-grant that leaks all guardian PII — the only current workaround is a privacy hole). These bite right now, independent of the upgrade.

## Wow opportunities

- **The team-reveal → coach claim loop** (J9-010 + cross-ref J7-016): the same "Nora is a Cardinal! Coach Pat, first game June 14" moment that delights the parent can carry the coach's claim link — the tier's highest-delight moment doubling as its coach-onboarding wire.
- **A per-team public page** (J9-001 + J9-009): one lightweight page (coach, schedule-with-location, live record) that serves the anonymous coach today and becomes the claim email's destination tomorrow — closing both the read-only gap and the season-end-recap gap at once.
- **Reuse the admin schedule card on the public surface** (lens delight note, 08-admin-schedule): the admin schedule card — coloured dots, score, full venue, amber POSTPONED chip — is the strongest card in the journey and is directly reusable on the public schedule with minimal change, closing J9-001's location + postponed gaps with zero new component work.
- **Score reporting as a coach** (J9-014): the rec-league coach self-reporting his own game's result, mirroring the tournament scorekeeper pattern — standings update without the admin keying every score.
