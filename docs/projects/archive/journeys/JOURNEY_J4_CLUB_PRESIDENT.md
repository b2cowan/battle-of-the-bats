## The journey at a glance

Robert bought the franchise model: the org creates and oversees teams, coaches operate them day-to-day — and the product *says so*, in plain words, on the rep-teams hub's own empty state. The remarkable headline of this walk is that **the contract mostly HOLDS where it matters most**: the roster contract is verified intact (the admin roster API is GET-only, and a repo-wide sweep found the only roster mutation outside the coaches portal is the org-owned tryout-accept intake), the org→team dues pipeline and the team→org payment-request rail are correctly franchised in both directions, tryouts are org-driven by design, and the admin calendar renders a literal "Read-only" label with zero edit affordances. But the oversight layer wrapped around that contract is part broken and part unguarded: a silent API side-door lets any owner/admin rewrite or delete a coach's events (a git-proven remnant of the very commit that hardened the franchise model), four admin routes are missing their role gate entirely — so a volunteer coach added as an org member can delete another team's medical-consent files or fire dues-reminder waves at every guardian in the club — and **17 pages are flat-out dead on Next 16**, including the page where a president checks on a volunteer coach's program and the entire premium coach accounting suite (J4-001, live-probe proven, fix-now).

The dead-page cluster deserves its own paragraph because it warps everything around it. The program-year overview — the flagship oversight surface of the top-tier plan — reads `params` synchronously off what is now a Promise, fetches `/api/admin/rep-teams/teams/undefined/program-years/undefined`, gets a 404, and permanently renders "Program year not found." for every user, while the same API with real ids returns 200. Three independent captures across three server restarts confirm it is not transient and not staging. Sixteen more files carry the identical legacy signature, spanning the admin rep-teams cluster and the premium coach money suite — the exact surfaces J2 deferred to Phase 4. And the failure mode compounds itself: a fetch *error* renders as *absence* (J4-002), with the sidebar simultaneously showing the team's nav group — the page tells a president his coach's program doesn't exist while the chrome insists it does.

The franchise audit (the dedicated section below — it formally closes the open "Rep Teams franchise model review" TODO) sorts the surfaces cleanly: aligned where designed, violated only through side-doors. Beyond the events API and the four ungated routes, coach-role members can *read* org-wide rep finance and every coach's email through the admin GET routes (the product's own help copy promises "Coaches see only their own team"), and the org accounting module quietly grants owner/treasurer full add/edit/void/transfer write on coach-owned team ledgers — the opposite of the read-only-oversight contract, and a recipe for coach-vs-board money disputes. None of these holes has a UI; that is exactly the problem. The UI keeps the promise; the API breaks it.

The money story has excellent bones — the UPCOMING PAYABLES panel (30/60/90-day lanes, overdue badges, action-needed chips) is the best oversight pattern in the product and belongs on the dashboard, and the Accounting overview's one-screen reconciled cash position is the strongest screen in the walk — and three coherence gaps on top of them. First, **the numbers can lie**: the Cost Allocations list's overdue count is hard-zero forever (the query never selects `due_date`), the board-facing "Org Headroom" headline is summed from at most 50 expense entries, and the page named "Budget vs. Actual" contains no actuals. Second, **the rails disagree**: a coach's payment request must survive an explicit org review, while the other team→org rail — Mark Paid on an allocation installment — moves real money on one unconfirmed click with no method captured, no review, no notification, and no undo anywhere in the lifecycle. Third, **billing doesn't match the confirm**: the charge Robert approves in the E4 modal never fires at that moment, the real charge lands later on an unrelated trigger, and archiving a team keeps billing it indefinitely. Above all three sits the walk's lone Blocker: the documented "+ Add Ledger" button plants a NULL-entity org ledger that makes every subsequent ledger-list GET spawn another "— General" and scatters real postings across the copies — data corruption from a first-class button (J4-020).

At club scale the IA still thinks it's a tournament product. The dashboard is seven doors and a rocket — zero numbers, zero attention items, the only CTA a tournament upsell (the club variant of J3's tournament-first skew, which also reproduces on the public homepage, the mobile bottom-nav, and the /home copy — all filed with cross-refs; notably, J3's locked-teaser contradiction did **not** reproduce on the club org, a verified non-finding). Robert cannot assign his own board from the Members UI (Treasurer exists in the API and the help docs but not the dropdown — and the PATCH silently demotes one to Staff), his invited VP-of-operations Admin is auto-redirected into tournament-land, and his public homepage leads with an empty tournaments apology fifty weeks a year while the rep program — half the club's identity — is publicly invisible behind a tryouts CTA that loops back to the homepage. The single biggest fix is the A1 rescue PR; the single biggest product move is the oversight layer the Club tier is actually bought for: a franchise health board, a whole-club financial summary, and a dashboard that opens with what needs Robert's attention instead of seven doors.
