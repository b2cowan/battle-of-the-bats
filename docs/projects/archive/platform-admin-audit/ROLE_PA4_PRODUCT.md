## The operator at a glance

The product operator is FieldLogicHQ's most broadly empowered non-super-admin role. Their nav renders every group — Command Center, Customers, Growth, Billing & Product, System — except the three super-admin-only entries: Platform Users, Dev Tools, and Retention. With write access to Plans & Pricing, Change Requests, Email Templates, Bulk Operations (module add-ons only), Early Access, Email, and Observability + Feedback, plus view-only on Customer Users and Organizations, they touch more surfaces than any other functional role.

A product operator's shift might open with a Feedback queue scan (triage inbound bug reports), pivot to Change Requests (approve or reject a pricing change from a draft someone submitted), then check the Observability dashboard for any spiking error groups, followed by a Plans & Pricing review of an upcoming plan limit configuration. That's four meaningfully different tools in a single morning. If product is also the growth owner (the `product` role inherits `manage_growth`), add Early Access and Email on top.

The console's breadth is both the product role's strength and its greatest day-one risk. The surfaces are individually well-designed, but there is no "start here" map for a new product hire. A new operator arriving for their first shift stares at 13 nav items across 5 groups and must figure out which ones they write, which ones are view-only, and which ones require a specific internal SOP before acting. The Help Hub has exactly one SOP section for the product domain (Plans & Pricing) and no SOP for Change Requests, Email Templates, Email blast management, or the Observability triage workflow.

The support seam is where product's breadth matters most: product holds `manage_product`, which means product CAN change feedback status and CAN resolve/ignore/snooze error groups. This is the write side PA2 found locked. Product's ability to close the loop is real, and the walk will confirm whether the end-to-end path is navigable or whether product discovers the same partial-coverage gaps PA2 did on the read side.

## Support-seam verdict

**Product CAN close the loop. The seam works end-to-end from the product side.**

Walking the concrete scenario — "a customer submits a bug report that scores won't save":

1. **RECEIVE.** Product navigates to Feedback. The Status column renders `StatusControls` (dropdown, not static badge) because `readOnly=false` for product. Product can immediately move the item from `new` → `triaged`. Audit log entry is written (`update_feedback_status`, actor email stamped). **Loop starts here — works.**

2. **DIAGNOSE.** If a `requestId` was captured, the "View related issue →" link appears in the feedback row's `<details>` panel. Product clicks through to the error group detail page. They see: route, stack trace (redacted), org slug, user email from sample events, first/last seen timestamps, occurrence count. `StatusControls` on the error group detail is enabled (not disabled as it is for support). **Full diagnostic data visible, controls enabled.**

3. **ACT.** Product can: (a) move the feedback status to `acknowledged`, (b) Snooze the error group (e.g. 24h if a deploy is imminent), or Resolve it once a fix is confirmed, (c) navigate to the related org via the org slug link in sample events, (d) view the customer's org detail. Product cannot directly message the customer or create a task — there is no such surface — but can add a note to the org record (via Organizations → org detail → Support tab) if they know to look there. No in-console notification path back to the customer exists for any role.

4. **CLOSE THE LOOP.** Product can mark the feedback `resolved` and mark the error group `resolved`. Both write actions complete. The audit log records both transitions with product's email. **The loop closes in the console.** The missing piece: there is no notification to the original submitter and no in-console "notify customer" affordance. The loop is complete *internally*; customer-communication must happen out of band.

**Root cause confirmation (code-verified):**
- `feedback/[id]/status/route.ts:19` — `requirePlatformPermission('manage_product')` — product holds `manage_product` → passes.
- `observability/[groupId]/status/route.ts:21` — same gate → passes.
- `isPlatformAreaReadOnly(auth.role, 'observability')` evaluates `false` for product → `readOnly=false` → StatusControls dropdown rendered and functional in both Feedback and error group detail pages.

**Where friction remains (not blockers):**
- No default `status=new` filter on Feedback means every session starts with a mixed list (PAP-004).
- No reverse back-link from error group → related feedback means the diagnostic pivot is one-directional (PAP-005).
- No SOP, so the product operator must already know the feedback→observability workflow on day one to execute it (PAP-010).
- No customer-communication affordance — closing the loop internally is possible; closing it with the customer requires out-of-band contact.

**Seam contrast with PA2:**
PA2 (support) cannot move feedback status at all — loop breaks at step 1. PA4 (product) can execute all four steps, but encounters UX friction at steps 2 (filter, back-link) and 4 (no customer notification). The seam is unidirectional: support escalates by leaving items in `new` and hoping product checks; product has no in-console mechanism to assign an item to support or notify them that an item has been addressed.

## Day-one verdict

**No — a new product hire cannot be productive on shift one without tribal knowledge.**

The specific blockers:
- The Help Hub has one Product SOP section (Plans & Pricing, 4 bullets). Change Requests, Email Templates, Email batch sends, Early Access disposition, and Observability/Feedback triage are undocumented. A product operator on day one has no procedural guidance for their five most consequential write surfaces.
- Bulk Operations opens in a state the product role cannot execute (default action type = billing). The product operator must discover their one valid action type without guidance.
- Customer Users shows an active Actions menu for product despite product having no write permissions there. Silent 403s on every action attempt.
- Plans & Pricing is the highest-stakes surface (live customer billing impact on every write) with the thinnest documented guardrails.

What works on day one: the nav is correctly role-scoped (eye icons are accurate), Feedback status changes work and are correctly enabled, Change Requests renders action buttons only for `canManageProduct=true`, and the Early Access list is self-explanatory with good copy templates built-in. The operator could handle triage work and Early Access on day one. Everything with higher stakes (pricing changes, email template edits, batch sends) requires SOP coverage that does not exist.

## Top 5 moves

1. **Fill the Product SOP gap (PAP-010 — Medium, cross-cutting).** Add a "Product Operator" role-path card to the Help Hub landing and populate SOP sections for Change Requests, Email Templates, Email batch authorization, and Observability/Feedback triage. This single effort unblocks day-one productivity across all of product's net-new write surfaces. Effort: one focused writing session in `lib/help-content/platform-admin.tsx`.

2. **Fix Customer Users silent-fail for product (PAP-003 — High).** Pass `canManageSupport=false` from the server page for product and render the Actions menu as view-only (matching the Observability StatusControls pattern). Eliminates the most jarring dead-end a product operator will hit when helping a colleague on a customer issue.

3. **Fix Bulk Operations default action type for product (PAP-002 — High).** Default to `module_addon_enablement` when the role cannot manage billing. Hide billing action types or label them "Requires billing access." Turns a confusing opening state into a purposeful one.

4. **Tighten email-template API write gates (PAP-001 — High).** Add `requirePlatformPermission('manage_product')` to the PUT and DELETE routes on `/api/platform-admin/email-templates/[key]`. The read risk (GET) is lower but the write path is a security gap — any session-holding role can modify production transactional email copy by direct URL.

5. **Default Feedback and Observability to actionable status filters (PAP-004 — Medium).** Default Feedback to `?status=new` and Observability to `?status=open`. This is the single daily-workflow change with the highest frequency impact for both product and support (PA2's PAS-006). Shared fix.
