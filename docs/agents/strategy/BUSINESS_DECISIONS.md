### 2026-07-20 — Free-first follows for ALL entity types: tournament + organization follows need no account (Unified Home Phase 6)
**Status:** Decided (ratified by owner 2026-07-20 in the Phase 6 mockup round — "lock it in")
**Decision:** The device-first, no-account follow model teams have always had **extends to both new Phase 6 follow types**: a fan can follow a **whole tournament** or an **organization** with one tap, signed out, stored on the device — exactly like team follows. Sign-in is a **benefit nudge, never a wall**: an account adds cross-device sync and (per the unchanged 2026-07-14 decision) score alerts. Device follows render on the signed-out Home/Scores exactly as device team follows do; the existing **explicit claim-on-sign-in offer** ("add this device's follows to your account?") extends to all three entity types — never silent merging. **Supersedes the Phase 6 plan's "account-only for v1" recommendation** (plan §4 updated same day). The owner **explicitly declined the all-account alternative** (requiring an account for team follows was rejected as too much re-architecture of the shipped device/pin system) — the my-team pin and device team follows work exactly as today. Boundaries kept: alerts remain account-only; a device tournament/org follow never seeds a my-team pin (independence is mockup Round F6, pending its own ratification); org-follow eligibility reuses the Phase 2 org-search predicate.
**Rationale:** One legible rule a fan can hold — "follow anything free; sign in to make it travel" — restoring the consistency the mixed model broke (an account gate for events beside no-account team follows read as arbitrary; owner flagged it on the rev-1 mockups). Extends the validated free-first acquisition posture (free-app conversion strategy): the QR-at-the-diamond moment stays frictionless for events, the thing casual fans most want to follow. Anonymous follows are public-data-only — no PII/PIPEDA surface. Accepted trade: anonymous follows stay invisible to any future organizer-facing follower counts (already true for teams); the sign-in nudge is the countermeasure.
**Affects:** Consumer follow UX (Phase 6 mockup artifact `d6a7c08b` rev 2 is the free-first spec); **no price, tier, plan-name, or gate change; no migration expected**.

### 2026-07-20 — Global app nav persists on public tournament pages at EVERY tier; no plan-gated hide (Unified Home IA — Phase 5, tournament nav merge)
**Status:** Decided (ratified by owner 2026-07-20 via decision prompt)
**Decision:** On public tournament pages (`/{orgSlug}/{tournamentSlug}/*`), the global consumer nav (**Home · Scores · Chat · Account**) will be **persistently present on every plan tier** — free `Tournament` through `Tournament Plus` — with **no dial-back, no plan-gated hide, no per-org opt-out**. The tournament's own pages move into a horizontally-scrolling top tab row under its branded header; the global bar stays as an always-present bottom (mobile) / top-strip (desktop) app-identity layer. Restraint for branded events is **cosmetic only** — the global bar renders **platform-neutral** (a thin, quiet, dark utility strip; only its active tab borrows the tournament's accent colour), **never** re-skinned to the org's brand and **never** removed for paid tiers. Existing paid branding is **unchanged**: `advanced_tournament_branding` (custom theme/logo/hero/name across the whole tournament page) remains a Tournament Plus feature; this decision only makes the **platform-neutral global nav** universal, layered around — not replacing — the branded space.
**Rationale:** The largest, most-branded events draw the most **one-time visitors**, who are the platform's best conversion targets into a retained, signed-in app user (the tournament-visitor → follow → chat → account loop). Gating the global bar away from paid/branded events would strip the acquisition loop from exactly the **highest-value traffic** — the opposite of the intent. A persistent, platform-neutral bar is consistent with a promise already made and priced under **G1 (2026-07-11)**: all tiers get the same one-app-shell treatment; organizers were told their event lives as a **branded SPACE inside the one app**, not a separately-badged product. The white-label promise is protected by keeping the bar visually quiet/neutral (it reads as "the app," like GameChanger's own app chrome around a team-branded header), not by hiding it. Full design + rationale in `docs/projects/active/UNIFIED_HOME_TOURNAMENT_NAV_MERGE_PLAN.md` + PM brief; source = 2026-07-20 12-agent nav-unification analysis.
**Affects:** consumer app IA / roadmap sequencing (a new Unified Home Phase 5, sequenced after the in-flight Phases commit + owner-test); positioning of what every tier gets on the shared app shell; **no price, plan-name, capacity-band, or gate change**; no migration; `advanced_tournament_branding` Plus gate **unchanged**.

### 2026-07-19 — Fan-search org discoverability REUSES the existing `is_discoverable` flag (one switch, no new column); tournament/team-search vs org `is_public` interaction left open (Unified Home IA — Phase 2 search)
**Status:** **Decided** — (a) reuse of `is_discoverable` ratified by owner 2026-07-18 via a build-kickoff decision prompt; (b) the `is_public`-vs-directory interaction ruled 2026-07-19 (**keep as-is — per-tournament opt-in stands**, see below). Both now Decided.
**Decision:** The new fan-facing Home search for **Organizations** reuses the **existing `organizations.is_discoverable` flag** (bool, default-on, already live on dev+prod) rather than introducing a new `list_in_directory` org column. This makes org **fan-search visibility and the coach→org link-picker visibility ONE switch, not two**, and Phase 2 ships with **no database migration**. The org-search eligibility predicate = `is_discoverable AND is_public AND account_kind≠team_workspace AND plan_id≠team AND subscription_status≠canceled` (a strict superset of the `/{orgSlug}` public page's own notFound gate, so a search hit never resolves to a dead link). Team search is scoped strictly to `list_in_directory` tournaments (name + event only, no PII).
**Rationale:** The DB house-rule schema check (decide column existence from live snapshots, never the plan) found `is_discoverable` already exists and already encodes "real, publicly-findable org" — the plan §3d had proposed a *new* column in apparent ignorance of it. Adding a second, near-identical visibility flag would invite drift (an org discoverable in one surface but not the other) and is redundant. Reuse also auto-excludes team-workspace shadow orgs (already provisioned `is_discoverable=false`). Owner picked reuse over "add the column as planned" and over "route to /strategy first." Full context in `docs/projects/active/UNIFIED_HOME_IA_REDESIGN_PLAN.md` §3d (updated) + `memory/project_unified_home_redesign.md`.
**`is_public`-vs-directory ruling (Decided 2026-07-19, owner via decision prompt): KEEP AS-IS — the per-tournament opt-in stands on its own.** The tournament + team directory/search keys on **per-tournament `list_in_directory`** (+ status + canceled-org exclusion) and deliberately does **not** gate on org-level `is_public`. The two flags stay orthogonal by design: org `is_public` = "do I publish an org home page?"; tournament `list_in_directory` = "should people discover this specific event?" No clarifying-note UI was added (owner chose the plain keep-as-is option, not keep-as-is-plus-note). **NO code change** — this ratifies the existing (and now team-search-inherited) behavior.

### 2026-07-14 — Fan score alerts require a signed-in account; anonymous alert opt-ins retired (following stays account-free)
**Status:** Decided (ratified 2026-07-14, owner-selected via decision prompt)
**Decision:** Receiving fan push alerts (game live/final scores, event news) now **requires a signed-in FieldLogicHQ account**. The **anonymous device-only alert opt-in path is retired for new opt-ins** at Slice 3 ship (existing anonymous opt-ins ≈ zero on prod; no migration program). **Anonymous following/browsing stays permanent and untouched** (locked Slice 1 posture). The **Tournament Plus gate on fan push is unchanged**; the G2 "alerts not offered by this event" legibility commitment is satisfied by one line on the notifications card naming the free-tier event(s).
**Rationale:** (1) **UX integrity** — the dual device+account model created two parallel settings stores; owner identified the trap (sign in, turn alerts off, an invisible device-level setting keeps notifying). One store makes that structurally impossible. (2) **Account-ladder strategy** — "get score alerts" is a materially stronger account-creation hook than "your follows travel," and account growth feeds the family/chat layers (G3/G4). (4) **Pre-revenue timing** — zero real anonymous-alert usage means the marketed "no account needed for alerts" behavior can be retired at no migration cost, same logic as G1's icon retirement. Trade-off accepted: one extra step (sign-in sheet) at the field-side moment of highest intent.

**Affects:** positioning, GTM.
**Handoff:** → `/marketing` persona-page emphasis and acquisition messaging centered on the mid-size dual-stream club.

### 2026-06-22 — Founding-Season conversion mechanics: September annual lock-in opt-in
**Status:** Proposed — *owner deferred 2026-06-22; revisit when a real founding cohort exists and the cliff date is firm.*
**Decision (proposed):** Convert the Jan 2027 billing cliff into a warm opt-in — an annual-commitment offer ("commit annual before the deadline, lock your founding rate") to orgs that have **actually run a real event**, with the offer **triggered by having run that event rather than a fixed calendar date** (tournament orgs in early autumn; season-based League/Club orgs later, once they've lived a season). **Not** a graduated discount ramp (rejected — it creates a second cliff).
**Rationale:** The Founding Season has no conversion mechanics; an event-triggered annual opt-in creates prepaid, committed founding customers without anchoring the cohort to a discount. **Deferred** because it is entirely forward-looking — there is no founding cohort yet, the cliff is ~7 months out, and the send is legally gated regardless; nothing is lost by deciding it closer to the event with more information. (Pricing & product review, 2026-06-20.)
**Affects:** lifecycle/email, billing.
**Handoff:** none yet (deferred). **When activated:** → `/marketing` the opt-in email/banner · → `/billing` annual checkout. **Blocked on:** CASL consent posture (signup captures opt-out, not express opt-in) — confirm lawful basis with counsel before any commercial send.

### 2026-06-22 — Open League + Club to managed early-access after a product-hardening sprint (~Aug 2026)

## Findings & context (not decisions — the evidence base behind the proposals above)

- **Competitive landscape (2026-06-20 review, 15 competitors):** The Canadian grassroots market is dominated by *free* software (RAMP, TeamLinkt, Spond) funded by payment take-rates (2.9–5% + fixed). US incumbents charge real money but are mostly USD-only, demo-gated, and opaque — a transparency/CAD gap FieldLogicHQ can exploit. Tournament Plus ($39) and League Plus ($89) are well-calibrated vs comparables; Club ($179) was under-priced; Premium Coaches Portal ($29) is defensible after FX (≈ TeamSnap Ultra).
- **Typical Canadian club size (2026-06-22 research, medium confidence):** Median target Club buyer runs **8–12 rep/competitive teams** (~10). Small 3–6, large 18–32+. ~85–90% of Club buyers exceed the old 3-included floor. By sport: hockey 8–12 (10–20 combined genders), softball 6–8 (9–13 with select), basketball 12–18, soccer 10–20, baseball 8–14. No official source — triangulated from real club rosters.
- **Adversarial review caution:** 10 of 16 aggressive "do-it-now" pricing moves were rejected as premature at zero customers. Theme: the Founding Season is a *product-validation* window; price-point fine-tuning becomes answerable only after 30–50 orgs run real seasons.
